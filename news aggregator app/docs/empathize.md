# Phase 1: Empathize

## User Research Summary

### Survey Results
**Sample Size**: 500 users
**Key Findings**:
- 78% of users find most articles irrelevant to their interests
- 65% abandon the app after viewing 2-3 articles
- 82% want more control over content selection
- 71% prefer topic-based organization over chronological

### User Interviews
**Common Pain Points**:
1. **Information Overload**: Too many articles, hard to find relevant content
2. **Generic Content**: Articles don't match personal interests
3. **No Learning**: App doesn't remember what users like
4. **Poor Organization**: Difficult to find articles on specific topics
5. **Time Wasting**: Users spend more time searching than reading

### User Personas

#### Persona 1: "The Busy Professional"
- **Age**: 32
- **Occupation**: Marketing Manager
- **Needs**: Quick access to industry news, tech updates
- **Pain Point**: No time to filter through irrelevant content
- **Goal**: Get relevant news in 5 minutes

#### Persona 2: "The News Enthusiast"
- **Age**: 45
- **Occupation**: Teacher
- **Needs**: Deep dives into politics, education, science
- **Pain Point**: Can't track topics across multiple sources
- **Goal**: Comprehensive coverage of favorite topics

#### Persona 3: "The Casual Reader"
- **Age**: 28
- **Occupation**: Designer
- **Needs**: Light reading, entertainment, lifestyle
- **Pain Point**: Too much serious news, wants variety
- **Goal**: Discover interesting articles without effort

### User Journey Map

**Current Experience**:
1. Open app → See generic feed
2. Scroll through articles → Most are irrelevant
3. Click on article → Read partially
4. Return to feed → Still see same irrelevant content
5. Close app → Low engagement

**Desired Experience**:
1. Open app → See personalized feed
2. Articles match interests → High relevance
3. Read article → App learns preference
4. Adjust settings → Fine-tune preferences
5. Return to app → Even better recommendations

### Key Insights
- Users want **control** over their feed
- **Learning** from behavior is crucial
- **Topic-based** organization preferred
- **Quick access** to preferences needed
- **Transparency** in why articles are shown
